# Salmon Coast Research Station & Surrounding Field Sites

## Region Overview

The Broughton Archipelago is a group of islands at the eastern end of Queen Charlotte Strait in Mount Waddington Regional District, British Columbia. This region is the unceded territory of the Musgamagw Dzawada'enuxw: the Dzawada'enuxw, Kwikwasut'inuxw Haxwa'mis First Nations, and the Gwawaenuk Tribe — part of the Kwakwaka'wakw peoples.

The archipelago's sheltered waters, complex coastline, and productive marine ecosystem make it one of BC's most important areas for herring spawn. Herring typically spawn in the intertidal and shallow subtidal zones on eelgrass, kelp, and rocky substrate throughout the archipelago during late February through April.

## Salmon Coast Field Station (SCFS)

**Coordinates:** 50.746229, -126.497876

**Location:** Echo Bay, west side of Gilford Island, Broughton Archipelago

**Mailing Address:** General Delivery, Simoom Sound, BC V0P 1S0

**Contact:** VHF Channel 16 "Salmon Coast", (250) 974-7177, info@salmoncoast.org

**Website:** https://salmoncoast.org

**Description:** A remote, off-grid research facility founded in 2006 by biologist Alexandra Morton, Sarah Haney, and Alan Calderwood. Originally Morton's homestead, it grew into a world-class field station studying the impacts of salmon farms on wild salmon populations. The station houses ~25 researchers with living cabins, wet/dry labs, a 40'x40' dock, workshop, gardens, and greenhouse. Powered by solar and micro-hydro, with gravity-fed spring water and wood stoves.

**Research Programs:**
- Sea louse monitoring (20+ years of data on juvenile wild salmon)
- Kelp ecosystem monitoring and mapping
- eDNA & viral transmission studies
- Forestry impacts on Pacific salmon
- Ecological modelling
- Salmon movement tracking
- Sea otter population monitoring

**Relevance to Herring Research:** The station sits in the heart of herring spawning territory. Researchers at SCFS have decades of local ecological knowledge about spawn timing, locations, and environmental conditions. The station could serve as a ground-truthing base for satellite-based herring spawn detection, providing validation data during the March-April spawning window.

## Echo Bay

**Coordinates:** 50.75000, -126.49472

**Type:** Unincorporated settlement

**Description:** A small community located on the west side of Gilford Island, immediately adjacent to the Salmon Coast Field Station. Home to the Simoom Sound post office and Echo Bay Resort. Has a small general store selling limited supplies and marine fuel. Accessible only by boat or float plane.

**Relevance to Herring Research:** The nearest community to SCFS, serving as a logistical hub. The bay itself and surrounding inlets are known herring spawning areas.

## Gilford Island

**Coordinates:** 50.72917, -126.37806

**Area:** 384 km² (148 mi²) — the largest island in the Broughton Archipelago

**Description:** Situated between Tribune Channel (north/east) and Knight Inlet (south). Traditional territory of the Kwikwasut'inuxw Haxwa'mis First Nation. Features include Port Elizabeth (large bay on south side), Viner Sound (northwest coast), Scott Cove, and several Indian reserves. Former villages include Gwayasdums (reoccupied) and Metap.

**Key Features:**
- **Port Elizabeth:** 50.67389, -126.47472 — Large protected bay on south side
- **Duck Cove:** 50.66167, -126.52167 — Head of Port Elizabeth
- **Maple Cove:** 50.67861, -126.46250 — North side of Port Elizabeth
- **Gilford Point:** 50.65167, -126.44056 — South entrance to Port Elizabeth
- **Dakiulis IR No. 7:** 50.73333, -126.56667 — Northwest tip (Islet Point)
- **Kyimla IR No. 11:** 50.73333, -126.15000 — East side (Trafford Point)

**Relevance to Herring Research:** The island's extensive coastline with numerous sheltered bays and coves provides prime herring spawning habitat. Port Elizabeth in particular is a large, protected embayment that should be a priority scanning target.

## Broughton Archipelago

**Coordinates:** 50.667, -126.500 (centre)

**Area:** Hundreds of islands and islets scattered throughout eastern Queen Charlotte Strait

**Description:** Named in 1792 by George Vancouver after William Robert Broughton, captain of HMS Chatham. Major islands include Gilford, Cormorant, Malcolm, Broughton, North Broughton, Bonwick, Crease, Eden, Hanson, Harbledown, Turnour, Village, Viscount, and Watson Islands. Cormorant Island (Alert Bay) is the most densely populated.

**Major Waterways:**
- Beware Passage, Broughton Strait, Clio Channel, Fife Sound
- Johnstone Strait, Kingcome Inlet, Knight Inlet
- Retreat Passage, Tribune Channel, Wells Passage

**Protected Areas:**
- Broughton Archipelago Provincial Park
- Broughton Archipelago Conservancy
- Echo Bay Marine Provincial Park
- Cormorant Channel Marine Provincial Park
- Burdwood Group Conservancy

**Relevance to Herring Research:** This is the core herring spawning area in the region. The myriad islands create sheltered channels and bays ideal for spawn. Conservation areas indicate high ecological value. The entire archipelago should be a primary focus for scanning.

## Knight Inlet

**Coordinates:** 50.68361, -125.87472 (mouth)

**Length:** ~125 km (78 mi) — one of the longest inlets on the BC Coast

**Width:** ~2.5 km average

**Description:** A major fjord opening into Queen Charlotte Strait at Memkumlis (Village Island). Fed by the Klinaklini River and Glacier from the Ha-Iltzuk Icefield. Subject to strong tidal currents and severe outflow winds. First charted by William Broughton in 1792. Kwak'wala name: *dzawadi*.

**Relevance to Herring Research:** The inlet's mouth and lower reaches near Gilford Island and the Broughton Archipelago are important herring migration routes. Spawning occurs in the more sheltered side channels and bays near the inlet's opening. The inlet connects the archipelago to the interior, influencing water temperature and salinity during spawn season.

## Kingcome Inlet

**Coordinates:** 50.95000, -126.20000

**Length:** ~35 km (22 mi) from mouth of Kingcome River to Sutlej Channel

**Width:** ~2.5 km average

**Description:** A fjord north and east of Broughton Island, connecting through Sutlej Channel to Queen Charlotte Strait. Includes side inlet Wakeman Sound, fed by the Wakeman River. Traditional territory of the Dzawada'enuxw First Nation and the Ḵwiḵwa̱sut'inux̱w Ha̱xwa'mis First Nation. Named after Captain John Kingcome, RN.

**Relevance to Herring Research:** Kingcome Inlet and Wakeman Sound are traditional herring spawning areas. The inlet's estuarine characteristics (freshwater from the Kingcome River mixing with marine water) create productive conditions for herring and their spawn substrate.

## Tribune Channel

**Coordinates:** 50.80528, -126.21611

**Description:** A channel separating the north and east sides of Gilford Island from the mainland. Bends around Gilford's east side with Thompson Sound opening eastward at the elbow. Southern end opens onto lower Knight Inlet. Named after HMS Tribune.

**Key Features:**
- **Viscount Island:** 50.68333, -126.20000 (approx) — In the channel near the Knight Inlet opening
- **Sergeaunt Channel:** 50.68444, -126.19417 — Separates Viscount Island from mainland
- **Pumish Point:** 50.71639, -126.18889 — NE end of Sergeaunt Channel
- **Nickoll Passage:** 50.67889, -126.24139 — West side of Viscount Island
- **Steep Head:** 50.66972, -126.18389 — South end of Sergeaunt Channel
- **tse'lxmedzes (beach):** 50.72778, -126.15000 — South shore, "crabapple trees on beach"

**Relevance to Herring Research:** Tribune Channel is a major marine corridor connecting Knight Inlet to the northern archipelago. The channel's shorelines and the waters around Viscount Island are herring migration and spawning habitat. The complex shoreline with numerous points and passages creates sheltered micro-habitats for spawn.

## Simoom Sound

**Coordinates:** 50.85000, -126.50000

**Description:** A sound on the east and north sides of the Wishart Peninsula, between Tribune Channel and Kingcome Inlet. Named after HMS Simoom. Captain George Vancouver anchored here in 1792. The Salmon Coast Field Station's mailing address uses Simoom Sound.

**Relevance to Herring Research:** The sound is adjacent to the research station and within the archipelago's herring spawning grounds. Its waters connect directly to Kingcome Inlet and Tribune Channel, making it part of the herring migration corridor.

## Thompson Sound

**Coordinates:** 50.78333, -126.05000

**Description:** A sound on the east side of Tribune Channel, east of Gilford Island. Site of the Kwikwasut'inuxw people's place of origin (wato/watu village). Fed by the Kakweiken River.

**Key Features:**

- **Cleave Point:** 50.74306, -126.12444 — South side entrance
- **Sackville Island:** 50.79139, -126.02333 — Small island near the head
- **wato (village):** 50.80000, -126.00833 — Kwikwasut'inuxw place of origin
- **Kakweken IR No. 4:** 50.80000, -126.03333 — Mouth of Kakweiken River
- **Thompson Sound locality:** 50.80000, -126.01667

**Relevance to Herring Research:** The sound's estuarine input from the Kakweiken River creates productive spawning conditions. Its location east of Gilford Island places it in the heart of herring territory.

## Wakeman Sound

**Coordinates:** 50.98472, -126.51528

**Description:** A side inlet of Kingcome Inlet, feeding north off the main inlet. Traditional home of the Haxwa'mis people. Fed by the Wakeman River.

**Key Features:**
- **Alalco IR No. 8 / Okwialis village:** 51.06667, -126.53333 — Mouth of Wakeman River
- **Dug-da-myse IR No. 12:** 51.03333, -126.51667 — Shore of Wakeman Sound
- **Kyidagwis IR No. 2:** 51.01667, -126.55000 — South end of sound

**Relevance to Herring Research:** An important subsidiary spawning area within the Kingcome Inlet system. The Wakeman River's freshwater outflow creates conditions favorable for herring spawn.

## Nearby Communities & Reference Points

### Alert Bay (Cormorant Island)
**Coordinates:** 50.58333, -126.93333
The main population centre in the Broughton Archipelago. Ferry terminal, hospital, supplies, and the U'mista Cultural Centre. Home of the 'Namgis First Nation.

### Port McNeill (Vancouver Island)
**Coordinates:** 50.59028, -127.08056
The nearest mainland-connected town with full services (groceries, fuel, hospital). Ferry to Alert Bay and Sointula. Main departure point for the Broughton Archipelago.

### Sointula (Malcolm Island)
**Coordinates:** 50.62944, -127.01667
Fishing community on Malcolm Island, west of the archipelago. Ferry connection to Port McNeill.

### Telegraph Cove
**Coordinates:** 50.54583, -126.83333
Small historic community on Vancouver Island's northeast coast. Whale watching and ecotourism hub. Boat launch access to Johnstone Strait and the archipelago.

### Port Hardy
**Coordinates:** 50.72250, -127.49278
Northernmost town on Vancouver Island. BC Ferries terminal for Inside Passage and Prince Rupert. Full services.

| Direction | Location | Coordinates |
|-----------|----------|-------------|

| **East** | Mainland inlets (Thompson Sound, Kakweiken River) | ~50.80, -126.00 |

**Approximate bounding box for scanning:** 50.55 to 51.10 N, -127.10 to -125.85 W

**Peak herring spawn window in this region:** Late February through mid-April (varies by year)

**Key spawning habitats to target:**
- Sheltered bays and coves throughout the archipelago
- Port Elizabeth (south Gilford Island)
- Tribune Channel shorelines
- Lower Knight Inlet bays
- Kingcome Inlet and Wakeman Sound (estuarine zones)
- Echo Bay and Simoom Sound (ground-truth accessible from SCFS)
- Beware Passage and Retreat Passage
- Fife Sound and Wells Passage
