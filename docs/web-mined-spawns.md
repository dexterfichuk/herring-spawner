### 2) CHEK News — Water turns turquoise as herring spawn swims to Ucluelet inlet: ‘I love that’

### 3) CHEK News — ‘Places that defy expectation’: Herring return in waves and find new places to spawn off Ahousaht

- Location: Ahousaht territory / waters around Tofino

### 4) UVic thesis — Analysis of the Pacific herring population spawning areas using satellite remote sensing
- URL: https://dspace.library.uvic.ca/items/0ecd7837-4041-414e-9a30-18d284cbc0b6/full
- Date: 2025
- Location: North-East Vancouver Island (NEVI) and southern BC waters
- Notes: thesis abstract says it used Landsat 5–9 and Sentinel-2 imagery from 1985–2024 to detect spawning events; useful technical background even though it is not a news article

### 5) DFO / Open Government — Pacific Herring Spawn Index Data
- URL: https://open.canada.ca/data/en/dataset/d892511c-d851-4f85-a0ec-708bc05d2810
- Date: record published 2021-06-02; modified 2026-03-24
- Location: BC-wide spawn index; includes section maps and survey resources
- Notes: not imagery, but an official spawning dataset and survey manual that can help anchor region names and historical activity

## Quick takeaways
- Best satellite-visible spawn examples in public sources are on the west coast of Vancouver Island (Ucluelet / Ahousaht / Tofino) in March.
- The UVic thesis confirms Sentinel-2/Landsat use for NE Vancouver Island and historical BC spawn detection.
- DFO’s spawn index dataset is the best public official companion source for regional/seasonal context.
